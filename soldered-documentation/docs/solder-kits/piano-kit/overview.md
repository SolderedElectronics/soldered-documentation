## Introduction


The **Piano Solder Kit** is an educational electronics kit that teaches you the fundamentals of soldering and electronics by building a fully functional capacitive touch piano. Built around the classic **ATmega328P microcontroller** (which comes pre-programmed and ready to assemble) it generates tones and outputs sound to an external speaker through the **LM386 Audio Amplifier** for optimal loudness. The board also features an **arpeggio mode** with adjustable arpeggio speed control and **multiple octave settings**.

## Contents of the kit

<CenteredImage src="/img/piano-solder-kit/piano-contents.jpg" alt="Piano Solder Kit Contents"/>

- **PCB:** Piano circuit board with clear component markings and 12 capacitive touch buttons, pre-soldered USB-C connector and 16 MHz crystal for normal microcontroller operation  
- **ATmega328P:** Pre-programmed microcontroller in DIP-28 package with IC socket  
- **LM386 Audio Amplifier IC:** In DIP-8 package with IC socket  
- **Switches:** Arpeggio mode, octave selector, and power ON/OFF  
- **Potentiometers:**  
  - 10 kΩ potentiometer for volume control  
  - 100 kΩ potentiometer for arpeggio speed control  
- **Resistors:**  
  - 12 × 1 MΩ  
  - 1 × 100 kΩ  
  - 5 × 10 kΩ  
  - 2 × 5.1 kΩ  
  - 1 × 100 Ω  
  - 1 × 10 Ω  
- **Capacitors:**  
  - 7 × 100 nF (ceramic)  
  - 2 × electrolytic 100 µF  
  - 1 × electrolytic 1000 µF  
  - 1 × electrolytic 680 µF  
- **Diodes:** 2 × 1N4007  
- **8 Ω Speaker:** For instant sound playback  
- **Pre-cut wires:** 2 × for soldering the speaker to the board
