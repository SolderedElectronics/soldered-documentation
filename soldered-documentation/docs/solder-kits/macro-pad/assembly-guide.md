On this page, we will guide you step by step on how to assemble this kit.

<WarningBox> Please read the instructions carefully and take all the usual safety precautions when soldering. If you're a beginner, be cautious! You’re holding a 300 °C tool after all — but we know you can do it. 🙂 </WarningBox>

<InfoBox> Before starting, make sure you have all the components at hand. You can find the complete components list in the [**Contents section on the Overview page**](/macro-pad/overview/#contents-of-the-kit). </InfoBox>

## Step 1: Solder the resistors to the PCB.

We'll start by soldering the resistors. In this kit there are 4 different resistor values, which can be identified using their **color codes**. Each resistor has colored bands that represent its resistance value. 

<InfoBox> **NOTE:** Resistors don't have polarity so you can place them either way! </InfoBox>

### Solder the 10k Ω resistors

You can identify the 10 kΩ reistors by looking at the color code image below, also they come connected in a pack of 8. The places where they need to be soldered on PCB are marked: **R5, R6, R10, R18, R19, R20, R21, R22**.

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/10k_resistors_pack.png" alt="10k resistor pack" caption="10k Ω resistor pack" width="500px"/>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/10k_resistors_placement.png" alt="Resistor placement" caption="Places where to solder 10k Ω resistors" width="500px"/>

### Solder the 470 Ω resistors

The 470 Ω resistors are marked on the PCB by with pins **R3, R4**.

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/470_resistor_colors.png" alt="470 Ohm resistors" caption="470 Ω resistor color code" width="500px"/>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/470_resistors_placement.png" alt="Resistor placement" caption="Places where to solder 470 Ω resistors" width="500px"/>

### Solder the 1k Ω resistors

Identify the 1k Ω resistors from the image below and solder them to the PCB on pins marked **R1, R2**.

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/1k_resistor_colors.png" alt="1k Ohm resistors" caption="1k Ω resistor color code" width="500px"/>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/1k_resistors_placement.png" alt="Resistor placement" caption="Places where to solder 1k Ohm resistors" width="500px"/>

### Solder the 2.2k Ω resistor

The last resistor needs to be soldered on pin marked **R16**.

<CenteredImage src="/img/dasduinoCORE-solder-kit/2.2kohm-resistor.jpg" alt="2.2k Ohm resistor" caption="2.2k Ω resistor color code" width="500px"/>

## Step 2: Solder the capacitors

This kit includes **two 0.1 µF ceramic capacitors**. Their placements on the PCB are marked **C1** and **C6**.

<InfoBox> **NOTE:** Ceramic capacitors are **not polarized**, so their orientation doesn't matter! </InfoBox>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/capacitors.png" alt="PCB capacitor placement" caption="Capacitors in the kit" width="500px"/>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/capacitors_placement.png" alt="PCB capacitor placement" caption="Capacitors placement on the PCB" width="500px"/>

## Step 3: Solder the pushbutton

Solder the 4 legs of a pushbutton on pin marked **SW1**, this will act as our RESET button.

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/pushbutton_placement.png" alt="Pushbutton placement" caption="Pushbutton placement on PCB" width="500px"/>

## Step 4: Solder the LED

Solder the LED on pin **D1**. Keep in mind that the LED's **cathode (-)** is on the **left pin**, and **anode (+)** is on the **right pin**!

<CenteredImage src="/img/stop-me-game/led.jpg" alt="Marked pins on LED" caption="Marked pins on LED" width="500px"/>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/led_placement.png" alt="LED placement" caption="LED placement on PCB" width="500px"/>

## Step 5: Solder the switches and rotary encoder

And for the final step, we'll solder the main mechanical components: **five MX switches** and **one rotary encoder**.

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/switches_highlited.png" alt="Image of switches and rotary encoder" caption="Required components" width="500px"/>

<CenteredImage src="/img/macro_pad/switches_placement.png" alt="Switches & encoder" caption="Placement of switches and rotary encoder on PCB" width="500px"/>

## All done!

Now, simply plug in via USB-C.
